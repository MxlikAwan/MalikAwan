The Ford Mustang was introduced in April 1964, creating a new class of automobiles known as the "pony car." Its sleek design, affordable price, and powerful performance made it an instant hit, with over a million units sold within the first two years. Since then, the Mustang has evolved through multiple generations, becoming an iconic symbol of American muscle cars and maintaining its legacy as a blend of style, power, and innovation.

TimeLine

1. 1964-1966: First Generation (1964 1/2 - 1973)

  • April 17, 1964: Introduction of the first Mustang at the New York World's Fair.
  • Instant success, with over a million units sold within the first two years.
  • Variants include the fastback, convertible, and coupe models.


2. 1967-1970: Second Generation

  • Introduction of the second-generation Mustang, often referred to as the "Mustang II."
  • Bigger dimensions and a more muscular appearance.
  • The Shelby GT350 and GT500 models were produced during this era.


3. 1971-1973: Third Generation

  • Continued evolution of the Mustang, though facing challenges due to stricter emission standards and oil crisis.
  • Larger and heavier design compared to earlier models.
  • This generation marked the end of the classic Mustang design era.


4. 1974-1978: Mustang II

  • The introduction of the smaller, more fuel-efficient Mustang II due to changing market demands.
  • Available as a coupe or hatchback.


5. 1979-1993: Fox Body Era

  • Return to a more performance-oriented design with the Fox Body platform.
  • More aerodynamic and lighter construction compared to previous models.
  • Variants include the SVO, GT, and LX models.


6. 1994-2004: Fourth Generation

  • Introduction of a completely redesigned Mustang, marking a departure from the Fox Body platform.
  • Retro styling cues reminiscent of the earlier Mustangs.
  • SVT Cobra and Bullitt editions were among the notable variants.


7. 2005-2014: Fifth Generation

  • Return to a more muscular, retro-inspired design.
  • Enhanced performance and handling.
  • Introduction of the Shelby GT500 and Boss 302 models.


8. 2015-Present: Sixth Generation and Beyond

  • Introduction of the sixth-generation Mustang with a more modern design and global appeal.
  • Available with turbocharged four-cylinder engines alongside V6 and V8 options.
  • Continual updates and special editions, including the GT350 and GT350R.
